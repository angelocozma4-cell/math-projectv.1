Complex Numbers

Explore imaginary and complex numbers, their operations, and real-world applications.

What is i?
i is the imaginary unit, defined as the square root of -1. i² = -1.
Complex Numbers
A complex number is written as a + bi where a is the real part and b is the imaginary part.
Example: 3 + 2i
Sample Problem
Simplify: (2 + 3i) + (4 - i)
Solution: (2+4) + (3i - i) = 6 + 2i
